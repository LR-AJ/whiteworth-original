Procedure for company in Mainland, Dubai

Define your Business Activity

Determine the business activity you want to carry out. Research the Dubai market or choose a trading activity of your choice. Thereafter, seek expert guidance to make an informed decision about your business activity. Your choice will determine the license and permits you will need. Once you finalize your business activity, you can proceed to the next step of reserving your trade name


Register your Trade Name

Choose a trading name that complies with the rules laid down by the government can be a tricky task, so it's best to seek assistance from an expert like a Whiteworth Expert. Once you've chosen a trading name, the next step is to reserve or register it


Select an appropriate Legal Structure

The legal structure of your company will determine ownership, the number of shareholders, and other important factors. The structure you adopt is important because it affects your level of accountability as an owner, the tax system, and the number of shareholders or partners. Before making a choice, it is crucial to thoroughly weigh the benefits and drawbacks of each legal framework. While there are a number of legal structures available in Dubai Mainland, including the sole proprietorship, civil company, and branch company, the limited liability corporation (LLC) is the most typical and popular choice for many business owners. An LLC offers its owners the benefit of limited liability protection and is simple to create and manage.


Applying for the License

The next stage is to submit an application for the necessary licence when you have finished the basic procedures of choosing your business activity, reserving your trade name, and figuring out the formal structure of your firm. The Department of Economic Development (DED) in Dubai must receive all required paperwork and documentation as part of the licence application procedure. In Dubai Mainland, the regulatory agency in charge of providing business licences is the DED. Depending on your line of work and the legal makeup of your business, different documents may be needed to apply for a licence. To make sure you have all the paperwork and information required for the licence application, it is advised to speak to a business consultant or a legal expert.


Applying for Visas

If you're a business owner looking to grow, you may need to hire staff from the UAE or outside. You must sponsor the visas of any foreign workers you intend to hire. Additionally, you must apply for the visas of your spouse and any dependents you intend to bring with you. It is crucial to remember that depending on your business activity and licence, establishing a mainland company in Dubai may necessitate additional licences and regulations.
