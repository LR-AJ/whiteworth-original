Overview of a Bank Account


A personal bank account in Dubai refers to an individual's account used for personal financial needs, excluding business purposes. It can be opened by both residents and non-residents of Dubai. However, it's important to note that some banks in Dubai only allow non-residents to open a Savings Account and not a Current Account. In such cases, non-residents may be issued a debit card instead of a checkbook to access their funds

Opening a Bank Account for non-residents

The following procedures are normally required for non-residents to open a personal bank account in Dubai:

  • Personal Presence: To open an account, the applicant must be present in person at the bank
  • Complete candidate Profile: The candidate must provide a curriculum vitae (CV) or profile outlining their qualifications and history
  • Passport copy: A copy of the applicant's passport is required. It should be of the page showing their admission into the UAE
  • Original Bank Statement: The applicant must provide an original personal bank statement for the most recent six-month period from their home country or any other nation where they have an account
  • Banking Reference: In most cases, a bank reference in original form is necessary. This reference can come from the applicant's corporate or personal account in their home country or any other nation where they have an account
  • Minimum Balance Requirement: Non-residents may need to have a minimum balance in their accounts with several banks in the United Arab Emirates. Each bank may have a different process and minimum balance requirements

Please be aware that these are broad recommendations and that different banks in Dubai may have different criteria and processes. In order to get precise and current information for creating a personal bank account as a non-resident, it is essential to get in touch with the chosen bank immediately.

Opening a Corporate Bank Account

When opening a corporate bank account in Dubai, the following documents and information are typically required

  • Copy of MOA/AOA: Memorandum of Association (MOA) or Articles of Association (AOA) of the company.
  • Trading License: A valid trading license issued by the relevant authorities.
  • Share Certificate: Share certificate indicating the ownership structure of the company.
  • Passport with UAE Entry Page: Passports of shareholders with the UAE entry page
  • Incorporation Certificate: Certificate of incorporation of the company
  • Existing or Prospective Clients: Information about existing or prospective clients, such as suppliers and buyers..
  • Banking Statements: Banking statements of shareholders for the last six months.
  • Utility Bill: Utility bill of shareholders showing proof of address
  • Corporate Legal Documents and Bank Statements: In the case of a shareholder having a company outside the UAE, corporate legal documents and bank statements may be required

It is important to note that bankers may request additional documents or information as needed during the account opening process.
