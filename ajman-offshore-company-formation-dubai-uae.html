Ajman Offshore Company Formation


As a trade-free zone in the UAE, Ajman Free Zone was first created in 1988. Since then, it has achieved self-government status, and in recent years, it has seen remarkable expansion. With its innovative facilities and numerous benefits, the Ajman free zone offers a setting where new firms can thrive.

It is an area that will house over 1500 businesses and has received significant investment, making it an excellent site for investors. Additionally, Ajman's strategic location and proximity to both the western and eastern markets are sure to draw in a large number of foreign investors from India and other nations. Additionally, the area includes four ports that serve over 100 vessels annually and two international airports, which improves accessibility in the free zone.

Conditions of Ajman Offshore Company Formation

  • The UAE business name selection criteria must be met by the proposed company names.
  • The minimum share capital required for Ajman offshore company formation is approximately AED 10,000
  • A detailed definition of the type of business activities that the offshore company will conduct must be provided
  • The incorporation documents must include the names and personal details of company shareholders, as well as the number of shares they hold.
  • The professional background of company secretaries and directors must be stated.
  • If shareholders are corporate bodies, they must present specific documents, including a board resolution to award power of attorney, a certificate of incorporation, a letter of good standing, and a memorandum of association
  • Documents for offshore business incorporation must be signed by company shareholders in front of an official from the Ajman free zone

Procedure for the Ajman offshore company formation Licence

  • All shareholders of the offshore company must provide valid passport copies
  • A copy of the UAE entry stamp page must be submitted
  • An original home bank reference letter is required from all listed shareholders.
  • Proof of shareholders' address in the form of utility bills or bank statements must be provided
  • Personal profiles or CVs of shareholders are required.
  • The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the UAE embassy must approve and certify all the paperwork used to incorporate an offshore business in Ajman
  • Several documents, including share certification, a registry of the company's shareholders, secretaries, and directors, articles and a memorandum of association, a signed certificate of incorporation, and a company stamp, will be supplied once the Ajman offshore company has been successfully established.

scope of Ajman Offshore Company Formation License

  • Consultancy and advisory services
  • Trading activities, including buying, selling, and holding company stakes
  • Compliant property ownership in accordance with UAE regulations
  • International service provision
  • Acting as a commission agent
  • General services
